The political globalization trilemma revisited: An empirical assessment across countries and over time

Online May 11, 2023 3

Introduction: Globalization can be defined as a phenomenon of increased economic integration among nations, characterized by the movement of products, people, and ideas across borders. As economic integration has spread across continents, political and financial institutions have evolved to enhance and regulate the global marketplace.

Section 1:The political globalization trilemma asserts that a government cannot simultaneously opt for deep international integration, national sovereignty and democratic politics, but rather is constrained to choosing two of the three at most.

Section 2: This paper employs cross-country panel data operationalizing the multifaceted three vertices of the trilemma. After explorative data analysis, we employ panel error-correction techniques to uncover the mutual interdependencies among the variables in the system.

Section 3: The econometric evidence supports the existence of a long-run relationship between economic integration, national sovereignty and democratic politics as postulated in the political globalization trilemma.

Conclusion: With the notion of political trilemma already hotly debated among political economists, the third wave of autocratization in recent years has led to concern about whether societies that choose hyperglobalization and national sovereignty are on the path to autocratisation.
